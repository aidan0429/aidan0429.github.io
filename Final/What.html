Explaining Universal Design.

What is it?

Overall, Universal Design is the method of creating products, experiences, and digital things that work for the most people possible, reguardless of age, ability, disability, or other barriers they may face. Universal Design is about designing in a way that everyone can participate without needing any extra features.

Principles of Universal Design

  • Equitable Use:
    • The design is marketable to all people even those with diverse abilities.
  • Flexibility in use:
    • Accommodates a wide range of abilities and preferences.
  • Simple and intuitive use:
    • Use of the design is easy to understand.
  • Perceptible Information:
    • The design communicates the correct information to the user easily.
  • Tolerance for error:
    • The design minimizes error and adverse actions from the user.
  • Low physical effort:
    • The design can be used with little phyiscal effort.
  • Size and space for approach and use:
    • Appropriate size and space if provided for interaction.

Diversity of Users

Different types of disabilties

  • Visual Impairment
  • Audio Impairment
  • Motor Disabilities
  • Cognitive Disabilities
  • Temporary Disabilities

Device differences

  • Mobile use
  • No keyboard/mouse
  • Screen size differences
  • Processing power
  • Internet accessability

Types of barriers online

While online, people face many different barriers that may make their experiences worse.

  • Tiny text and or Low contrast
  • Videos without captions
  • Images without alt-text
  • Confusing navigation
  • Color communication
  • Keyboard accessability
  • and more....

Conclusion:

In conclusion, Universal Design is a ever changing field which helps all users have the same level of web usability that others have. From making proper semantic structure, to allowing keyboard access to a website, Universal Design is all of this.
