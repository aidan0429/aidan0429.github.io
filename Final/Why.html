Why Does Universal Design Matter?

Who are we designing websites for?

When designing websites, we must consider creating the website for all potential users. This includes people with disabilities, cultural differences, device differences, and differing levels of digital experience.

Differences between users

  • Users will all be aged differently.
  • Users may have varying levels of disabilities
  • Users may have cultural or linguistic differences
  • Users may have varying levels of digital accessessability.
  • Users may have varying levels of experience using technology.

Inclusion and equal access worldwide

A main role of Universal Design is making sure that no matter where in the world you live, you have access to the same usage as everyone else. This means considering different levels of internet access, connectivity, internet speeds, and device types when building for Universal Design.

Making things easier for people

  • Clear navigation

    • This means making sure that users can easily find what they are looking for without confusion.

  • Readable text and strong contrast

    • This means using fonts and colors that are easy to read and have a good contrast score.

  • Accessible forms

    • This means making sure that all forms on the website are accessible through screen readers and tab controls.

  • Images and videos with captions/alt-text

    • This means providing all images with descriptive alt text.

  • Keyboard accessibility

    • This means ensuring that all interactive elements can be accessed using a keyboard.

Laws and regulations around accessibility

All around the world, countries impose laws and regulations in order to ensure that websites are accessible to all users. This includes the Americans with Disabilities Act in the United States, and more laws and regulations from other countries.

Making sure to not exclude anyone

The most important part of Universal Design is making sure that all users are included. This means considering all barriers that users may face, and ensuring that there are solutions for these barriers.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Universal Design is essential for ensuring that all users are able to access the web equally. By considering all the needs of the users, we can ensure that no one is excluded and that everyone has the same level of accessibility across the web.
